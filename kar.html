Karate

Before Everything

Karate came before table tennis. It was my first structured discipline — the first place I learned what it meant to train something deliberately, repeat it until it became instinct, and measure progress not by talent but by consistency.

Stepping onto the mat for the first time, I encountered a kind of silence that sport rarely offers. Focused, intentional, and completely present.

📷 Image 01

What It Built

The dojo teaches you to be calm inside chaos. Every kata, every sparring round, every belt grading is a lesson in composure under pressure. That stillness — the ability to stay controlled when the situation demands a reaction — is something I've carried long after stepping off the mat.

Self-defence is the obvious benefit. But the deeper one is attitude. Karate trains you to not flinch, to not back down, and to respond rather than react.

📷 Image 02

The Carry-Over

The discipline from those early years of training flows directly into how I approach difficult problems today — whether it's a tight match in table tennis, a blunder in chess, or a bug that won't cooperate at 2am.

The posture is always the same: stay composed, break it down, move forward.

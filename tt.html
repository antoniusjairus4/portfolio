The Beginning

Table tennis wasn't just a sport I picked up — it became the frame through which I understood competition, pressure, and the cost of losing. I started young, and from the first time I held a paddle, something about the pace of the game felt right.

It demanded total presence. No room for distraction. Every rally is a micro-decision under time pressure — the kind of environment that builds a certain kind of mind over years.

📷 Image 01

The Championship

Winning the Tamil Nadu State-level championship once is a milestone. Winning it twice is a statement. Each title came from months of preparation, early mornings, and matches where the margin between victory and defeat was razor thin.

My favourite players — Fan Zhendong, Ma Long, and Félix Lebrun — represent the ceiling of what this sport can be. Watching them play shaped how I think about technique, mental toughness, and what it means to perform when it truly matters.

📷 Image 02

What It Taught Me

Losses hit hard. That's never changed. But every loss is a dataset — something to analyse, correct, and never repeat. Table tennis taught me that emotion and execution are two separate things, and the best players keep them that way.

It's a lesson I apply in code, chess, and every high-pressure situation. Stay composed. Break it down. Move forward.

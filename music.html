Music — Keyboard

The Counterweight

Everything else in my life runs on competition and pressure. Music is the exception. When I sit at the keyboard, there's no opponent, no rating, no deadline. It's just sound and the space between notes.

That's exactly why I keep coming back to it. The absence of stakes is the point.

📷 Image 01

What It Does

Playing keyboard requires a different kind of focus than sport or code. It's less about problem-solving and more about listening — to the melody, to the timing, to what the piece is asking for.

That shift in mode is genuinely restorative. After a long session of debugging or analysis, music resets something in the way nothing else quite manages.

📷 Image 02

The Practice

Progress on an instrument is slow, nonlinear, and humbling. You can spend an hour on eight bars and still not have them right. There's a lesson in that worth sitting with.

I don't play to perform. It's a private pursuit — and that's deliberate. Not everything needs an audience. Some things exist just to keep you sane and whole.
